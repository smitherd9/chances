How the score is calculated

What are the Chances? calculates your overall chances of food poisoning by gathering inspection data from the NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene.

Each restaurant is given an inspection score and a letter grade by NYC DOHMH.

WATC? gets the inspection scores for each restaurant, generates an average score, and also generates an average score based on the number of times a particular letter grade was received. It then codenses these two scores to a number from 0 to 10 with 0 being the lowest chance of food poisoning (or no data available) and 10 being the highest.

Users can search by zipcode, type of cuisine, specific restaurant or any combination of these three. You can learn more about how NYC DOHMH grades and scores here:
